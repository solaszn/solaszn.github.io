ACCREDIT

Designing the brand and product experience of a mobile loan application for both individuals and businesses

User Experience Design
Brand Design
Overview

Quick instant loans have become crucial for individuals and small businesses as more and more people turn to digital loan solutions for their financial needs. With established lenders , Accredit sets itself apart using a fair credit system and a simplified user experience.

I led the design of the brand identity and the first version of the product which will launch on mobile platforms.

The challenge

The financial landscape has witnessed a significant shift towards digital loan solutions in recent years, yet many existing loan apps burden users with complex user experiences and impose exorbitant interest rates, tying users to heavy financial obligations.

My goal was to simplify the loan application, approval and repayment process and design an identity to drive growth with trust and credibility

Research - User

Let’s start with the question..

“What do people think about loan apps?”

Surveys provided quantitative data, while calls and interviews offered qualitative insights directly from users. Sentiment analysis of tweets provided real-time user feedback. This comprehensive approach helped us gather extensive scope and insights, forming a solid foundation for the design process.

Research - Market
“What differentiates existing solutions?”

Research encompassed in-depth analysis of the market and competitor offerings allowed us to identify crucial market gaps and pinpoint opportunities for gaining a competitive edge. With valuable insights, we strategically positioned Accredit to address the unmet needs and pain points of borrowers.

Key Findings

Unclear Communication

Cumbersome loan application requirements and unclear terms and conditions were common sources of frustration for users.

Repayments

Many people cited confusion over repayment schedules and were frustrated with aggressive tactics used by some lending apps.

High Interest Rates

Borrowers are troubled by exorbitant interest rates of major lenders, often leading to debt traps and financial stress.

Planning

By organizing features, pages and functionalities logically in a workflow, we simplified complex navigations and processes for a seamless user journey. This process helped optimize key features, enhance the experience end to end and addressed pain points efficiently.

Information architecture
Styles and components

The brand was positioned to be warm, simple and use a direct tone. The approved brand mark, colors and typefaces were used to achieve visual consistency on both digital and physical mediums. In doing so, I created a cohesive experience across various touch-points to drive loyalty among users. For more insights on the brand design process, see this presentation.

Using the brand elements and an atomic design approach, the style guide and a library of reusable components form the building blocks of the design system.

Branding & UI Kit
Iterations

Through testing and successive iterations on feedback, the final design addressed user pain points, improved usability, and delivered a solution that resonated effectively with our target audience. This iterative approach results in a product that is intuitive and practical for borrowers.

Home Page Iterations
Usability study

For instance during testing, participants often got stuck on the loan agreement page. It was a key requirement from the client to inform users of the risks involved. To fix this, the secure PIN was used as a universal authenticator on the app and necessary documents were instead linked on the page.

A resulting unmoderated testing session showed that they all completed the flow relatively with ease on the updated design.

Solution

The meticulous design process ensured the final designs were satisfactory. By embracing a human-centered approach, conducting usability testing, and iterating on feedback, we fine-tuned every detail. A commitment to the product vision and adherence to best design practices were critical

This all contributed to a product with a user-friendly interface, strong visual appeal, and seamless functionality that resonates with users.

Kyc Flow
Loan types

With three distinct loan types—payday, personal, and business— users have greater control over their borrowing choices. Each loan type was designed to offer clarity on its purpose, terms, and associated risks, enabling users to make informed decisions aligned with their specific needs.

Multiple Loan Offerings
Credit

Users can boost their credit scores and unlock enhanced borrowing capabilities by fulfilling their debt repayments, fostering financial trust and rewarding responsible financial behaviour. This aligns with our mission to promote transparent and ethical lending practices.

Credit & Reward system
Repayments

Reminders across multiple channels, including SMS, email, and in-app notifications increase the likelihood of timely repayments. Furthermore, diverse repayment methods such as USSD, bank transfers, and card auto-debit, cater to users' individual preferences and ease the repayment process.

Repayment flow
Handling errors

By providing clear error messages, guidance, and accessible support channels, users can understand the situation and recover from setbacks effectively, transforming them into confident borrowers who view our platform as a trustworthy and supportive financial partner.

Late repayments
Going forward

As the product evolves following go-to-market and user needs get more and more complex, a few possible next steps to consider are:

  • Credit report: Increase borrowing power of users across other platforms on request to access, in line with the Credit Reporting Act 2017.

  • Custom internal dashboard: Implement back-office loan management system to replace rigid white label software in use currently.

  • Collaborations: Explore partnerships with financial institutions to offer diverse financial products to users such as BNPL and savings accounts.

Outcomes

This project came with its challenges but thorough research and testing informed the design process and ensured a product that meets the users needs. The branding and design system establishes trust and recognition. Together with the user experience and focus on error handling, Accredit is set on a path for sustainable growth in the market.
